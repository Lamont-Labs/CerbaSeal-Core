CerbaSeal — External Reviewer Portal

A structured review surface for technical evaluators, security reviewers, and pilot decision-makers.

CerbaSeal is a deterministic execution enforcement spine for AI-assisted workflows. It sits between a decision system and an execution system, returning ALLOW, HOLD, or REJECT with a hash-linked evidence trail.

What problem it solves

AI systems can propose consequential actions. They should not be able to authorize them.

As AI systems move from generating text to proposing actions — in workflows, transactions, accounts, and operations — most governance tools observe and log after the fact. CerbaSeal enforces the execution boundary before the action occurs.

Every proposed action passes through a deterministic gate. The gate checks policy reference, provenance, logging readiness, authority class, approval requirements, control state, and trust state. The result is a structured decision envelope — ALLOW, HOLD, or REJECT — with a hash-linked evidence trail that can be replayed, exported, and audited.

CerbaSeal enforces authority. It does not determine whether an action is correct. Contextual correctness remains the responsibility of upstream decision systems and human reviewers.

Why this matters

Decision control

Every proposed action must pass a complete invariant check before execution. There is no path around the gate.

Auditability

Every outcome — including REJECT and HOLD — produces a hash-linked evidence bundle. Nothing is silently discarded.

Replayability

Stored evidence can be replayed through the gate. Replay must match the original outcome deterministically.

Non-bypassability

Gate results are registered in a module-private WeakSet. Externally constructed results are rejected by the evidence layer.

Human authority preservation

AI actors cannot authorize their own AI-sourced proposals under any condition. Human approval is a structural requirement, not a configuration flag.

CerbaSeal System Identity

Mark and Name

The CerbaSeal mark is a three-headed guardian representing the three enforcement outcomes: ALLOW, HOLD, and REJECT. The keyhole in the shield body represents controlled execution — nothing passes this boundary without authorization. The name combines Cerberus (the guardian of a threshold) with Seal (the enforcement act of sealing an outcome with evidence).

All claims in this portal are backed by code, tests, or visible demo output.

Governance vocabulary — invariant → enterprise mapping

CerbaSeal's 12 enforcement invariants map directly to enterprise governance control categories. Use this table when mapping CerbaSeal to a regulatory framework, audit program, or governance committee requirement.

InvariantGovernance CategoryControl Obligation
INV-01Policy GatingEvery consequential action must operate under an identified, versioned policy authority. Ungoverned execution is not permitted.
INV-02Provenance AssuranceEvery action must carry a complete, traceable reference to the decision source — including model version, rule set, and source hash.
INV-03Human Authority GateWhere human authorization is required, it must be present, bound to the specific request, issued by an authorized authority class, and affirmed. Missing or invalid approvals block release.
INV-04Audit Readiness ObligationConsequential actions may not proceed unless the audit logging precondition is satisfied.
INV-05AI Authority BoundaryAI systems are permitted to propose actions. They are prohibited from authorizing or approving their own proposals.
INV-06Gate IntegrityAll consequential actions must route through the governed enforcement gate. Fabricated governance artifacts are rejected.
INV-07Decision Record IntegrityGovernance decisions, once issued, must be treated as immutable governed artifacts.
INV-08Control Currency RequirementFor sensitive workflows, critical controls must be current and valid at the time of release.
INV-09Trust Validation GateExecution may not proceed without a valid, current trust state declaration.
INV-10Prohibited Use EnforcementRequests classified as prohibited use must be rejected immediately. No exception path. No escalation path.
INV-11Input IntegrityMalformed requests and unrecognized action classes fail closed. The system does not guess intent or apply defaults for unknown inputs.
INV-12Proposal BindingThe action class in the governing request must exactly match the action class in the underlying proposal. Mismatches are rejected.

What CerbaSeal enforces vs. what it does not

CerbaSeal enforces authority and process — not correctness, safety, or intent. This separation is critical for regulatory mapping.

Enforces
  • Whether the required authority is present
  • Whether the request is structurally sound
  • Whether audit readiness is declared
  • Whether the action class is recognized
  • Whether prohibitions are applied
  • Whether AI systems stay within their authority role
Does Not Enforce
  • Whether the action is the right one to take
  • Whether the reasoning is sufficient
  • Whether the approver was appropriately senior
  • Whether harm could result from an allowed action
  • Whether policy content is appropriate for context
  • Whether the AI's proposal was accurate or safe
