# CerbaSeal — Pricing Evidence and Commercial Model

**Audience:** Partner sales leads, partner principals, Lamont Labs commercialization  
**Version:** v0.1.0 | June 2026  
**Classification:** Partner Confidential — Authorized Recipients Only

---

## Overview

The pricing model separates discovery, pilot, annual license, implementation services, and channel economics. Mixing those together creates confusion because a paid pilot often includes substantial services, while an annual license is primarily software and support.

The recommended pricing posture is: **do not name a final number until pilot scope, support expectations, and partner responsibilities are defined.** Use the tiers below as structured discussion anchors, not fixed prices.

---

## Part 1 — Pilot Pricing Tiers

A CerbaSeal pilot should be priced as a guided governance validation engagement. It is not a free trial and it is not a full production rollout.

| Tier | Name | Target Range | Duration | Primary Deliverable |
|------|------|-------------|----------|---------------------|
| 0 | Discovery / Readiness | USD/EUR 5K–15K | 1–2 weeks | Fit assessment, workflow candidate, deployment path, pilot scope |
| 1 | Validation Sandbox | USD/EUR 15K–35K | 30–45 days | Synthetic or representative workflow validation, starter kit, evidence sample |
| 2 | Controlled Workflow Pilot | USD/EUR 45K–75K | 45–90 days | One production-adjacent workflow, guided deployment, evidence package, support window |
| 3 | Regulated Evidence Pilot | USD/EUR 75K–150K | 60–120 days | More stakeholder review, stronger evidence package, broader operational validation |
| 4 | Strategic Anchor Deployment | USD/EUR 150K–250K+ | 90–180 days | Multi-workflow or strategic partner deployment with extended implementation and roadmap input |

**Pilot pricing includes:** Discovery, workflow mapping, deployment support, validation, training, support window, and evidence closeout. These are services — the pilot fee should not be directly compared to an annual software license, which is services-light.

## Part 2 — Annual Licensing Model

| Scenario | Annual Software Range | Rationale |
|----------|-----------------------|-----------|
| Conservative | USD/EUR 35K–75K | Anchored to lower-end AI governance, policy enforcement, and early controlled deployment |
| Realistic | USD/EUR 75K–150K | Reflects runtime enforcement, evidence generation, customer-controlled deployment, and partner support |
| Aggressive | USD/EUR 150K–300K+ | Appropriate for regulated, multi-workflow, high-usage, or strategic deployments with broader operational value |

**Annual license excludes one-time implementation.** The pilot fee should not be judged against the annual license directly; the pilot is services-heavy. Annual licensing is primarily software, support SLA, and version access.

## Part 3 — Pricing Assumptions

| Assumption | Commercial Effect |
|-----------|------------------|
| Pilot pricing includes services | Discovery, mapping, deployment, validation, training, support, and evidence closeout are bundled into the pilot fee |
| Annual license excludes one-time implementation | Annual licensing should not be judged against pilot fee directly |
| Partner-led delivery changes margin structure | Partner may earn services margin, referral fee, resale margin, or revenue share depending on role |
| Configuration maturity increases pricing power | Policy builder, workflow wizard, and governance packs reduce implementation cost and support higher repeatability |
| Security review increases enterprise readiness | Third-party security review (planned v0.2.0) should improve procurement confidence and support higher pricing |

## Part 4 — Channel and Partner Economics

CerbaSeal is likely to reach early regulated customers through advisory and implementation partners. The partner is not merely a reseller — they may own client relationship, regulatory framing, implementation support, pilot scoping, and ongoing account management.

| Model | When It Fits | Revenue Logic |
|-------|-------------|---------------|
| Referral | Partner introduces customer but Lamont Labs sells and delivers | Referral fee or commission; lowest partner workload |
| Reseller | Partner sells CerbaSeal into client account with defined discount or margin | Partner margin reflects sales ownership but not full implementation burden |
| Implementation partner | Partner configures, deploys, trains, and supports customer | Partner earns services margin; Lamont Labs earns software license and/or support fee |
| Strategic EU channel | Partner has regional relationships, regulatory expertise, and possible exclusivity | Requires written commitments, territory, duration, minimum activity, exit terms, and IP protection |

**Benchmark context:** Public AWS Marketplace research reports professional-services gross margins of 25%–45% and average software resale gross margins around 13%. This supports a channel structure where partners earn meaningful services revenue while Lamont Labs protects product IP and core licensing value.

## Part 5 — Exclusivity Considerations

| Question | Why It Matters |
|----------|----------------|
| What triggers exclusivity? | Avoid granting exclusivity before a pilot, minimum activity, or signed client commitment |
| What keeps exclusivity active? | Define minimum outreach, pilot targets, revenue milestones, or time-based review |
| What happens if no pilot occurs? | Prevent indefinite lock-up without commercial progress |
| What territory and market are covered? | EU-only language must be precise; consider sector boundaries if needed |
| What rights remain with Lamont Labs? | Protect core IP, non-EU markets, inbound opportunities, and roadmap control |

**Commercial posture:** Remain open to exclusivity discussion, but require written terms, clear commitments, limited duration, exit conditions, and protected IP ownership.

## Part 6 — Pilot Commercial Structure

### Recommended First Pilot Package

| Component | Included |
|-----------|---------|
| Discovery and readiness | Workflow candidate review, client readiness check, deployment mode selection, success criteria |
| Workflow mapping | Actor roles, approval path, proposed actions, policy pack, GovernedRequest mapping |
| Deployment support | Docker Compose or Node.js Direct setup, config/policy validation, health check, audit-chain verification |
| Scenario validation | ALLOW, HOLD, and REJECT test cases against representative workflow |
| Evidence closeout | Proof export, proof verification, evidence review session, pilot outcome summary |
| Support window | Bounded support hours, partner-led Tier 1/2 support, Lamont Labs Tier 3 escalation for core issues |

### Commercial Model Options

| Model | How It Works | When It Fits |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| Fixed technical fee | Lamont Labs receives a fixed fee per pilot; partner prices client separately | Best for early pilots when client pricing is uncertain but Lamont Labs support burden must be bounded |
| Percentage split | Lamont Labs receives agreed percentage of client pilot fee | Best when partner owns client relationship and pricing is transparent |
| Hybrid | Base technical fee plus percentage upside | Best for balancing support protection with upside from larger engagements |
| License plus services | Client pays software license plus partner implementation services | Best after first pilot once scope and repeatability are clearer |

### Negotiation Posture

| Topic | Recommended Stance |
|-------|-------------------|
| Pricing | Do not name a final number until pilot scope, support expectations, and partner responsibilities are defined |
| IP | Lamont Labs retains CerbaSeal core IP. Client-specific workflow artifacts and derivative improvements need written treatment |
| Support | Bounded, scheduled, and role-defined. Avoid open-ended availability |
| Exclusivity | Open to discussion only with written terms, activity commitments, limited duration, and exit conditions |
| Pilot success | Define before work begins: evidence package, operational sustainability, client value, and next-step clarity |

---

## Part 7 — What Should Not Be Promised

Do not promise, commit to, or allow the client to assume:

- Production certification or formal regulatory compliance certification
- Unlimited custom development during or after the pilot
- 24/7 support without written scope and defined response SLA
- That CerbaSeal replaces the client's existing compliance, identity, workflow, or AI systems
- That the gate validates AI model quality, correctness, or bias — it validates governance process
