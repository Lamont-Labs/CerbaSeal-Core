# CerbaSeal — Adoption Roadmap and Build Priorities

**Audience:** Lamont Labs, partner principals, pilot planning leads  
**Version:** v0.1.0 | June 2026  
**Classification:** Partner Confidential — Authorized Recipients Only

---

## Framing

The highest-leverage roadmap items are the ones that reduce implementation friction, partner dependency, and founder involvement without changing the core enforcement model. Additional invariants and more tests are useful, but the commercial bottleneck is repeatable delivery.

The goal is **guided independence**: clients retain control of environment and workflow; partners and Lamont Labs provide a repeatable path for deployment, mapping, configuration, validation, evidence review, and escalation support. Routine operation should not depend on a single founder or implementation engineer.

## Build Priorities

| Priority | Build Item | Why It Matters | Expected Commercial Impact |
|----------|-----------|---------------|--------------------------|
| 1 | **Policy pack builder** | Reduces the need for partners to hand-author JSON | Moves configuration from developer work to guided governance setup |
| 2 | **Workflow mapping wizard** | Turns discovery into a repeatable process | Reduces consulting effort and founder support during first pilot |
| 3 | **Governance pack library** | Provides templates for finance, insurance, KYC, claims, and AI agent workflows | Reduces blank-page friction and improves buyer relevance |
| 4 | **Replay and evidence explorer** | Makes audit and evidence review accessible to non-engineering stakeholders | Improves compliance, partner, and executive understanding |
| 5 | **Partner deployment automation** | Further reduces setup time and support load | Raises partner readiness and lowers onboarding cost |
| 6 | **Security review package** | Supports procurement, CISO review, and enterprise readiness | Improves trust and accelerates review process |

## Founder Independence Metrics

The objective is to shift from founder-supported to partner-led delivery across every routine engagement dimension.

| Dependency Area | Current Direction | Target Outcome |
|----------------|------------------|----------------|
| Workflow mapping questions | Workflow mapping workbook and future wizard | Partner can map common workflows without Tier 3 support |
| Policy authoring | Templates, policy pack builder, validation scripts | Configuration becomes guided setup rather than source-level work |
| Deployment troubleshooting | Docker Compose path, health endpoint, deployment checklist | Partner resolves routine deployment issues |
| Evidence interpretation | Evidence explorer, closeout template, training material | Client and partner can review proof package without founder-led explanation |
| Commercial scoping | Pilot tiers, pricing framework, and success criteria | Partner can frame pilot offer consistently |

**Metric direction:**
- Typical founder hours per pilot → Move from founder-supported to partner-led, with Tier 3 reserved for core changes or unresolved defects
- Configuration burden → Move from JSON authoring by trained partners to guided policy builder and validation
- Deployment burden → Move from documented deployment to automated deployment with health checks and proof validation
- Evidence explanation burden → Move from founder-led explanation to visual evidence explorer and closeout template
- Partner readiness → Move from "trained partner can deploy/configure" to "partner can lead pilots independently"

## Overall Assessment

> CerbaSeal has moved beyond a pure technical proof. The credible near-term path is a controlled workflow pilot, priced as a guided governance validation engagement, delivered through a partner-supported operating model, and strengthened by productization work that reduces configuration and deployment friction.

The core commercial question is no longer simply whether CerbaSeal works. The evidence supports technical credibility. The next question is whether CerbaSeal can be delivered repeatedly with bounded support, partner enablement, clear pricing, and a customer experience that does not depend on the founder being present for every operational detail.
